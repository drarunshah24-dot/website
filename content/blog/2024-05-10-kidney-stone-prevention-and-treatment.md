Kidney stones (known locally as *Patthari*) are one of the most common and painful urological conditions affecting adults. At National Urology Center in Janakpur, we treat hundreds of patients every year suffering from urinary calculi. Understanding why kidney stones form and how modern laser surgery can eliminate them is the first step toward effective relief.

## Why Do Kidney Stones Form?

Kidney stones develop when urine contains more crystal-forming substances—such as calcium, oxalate, and uric acid—than the fluid in your urine can dilute. Common risk factors include:

- **Dehydration:** Not drinking enough water, especially during hot summers in the Madhesh region, leads to concentrated urine.
- **Dietary Factors:** High intake of sodium (salt), animal protein, and oxalate-rich foods (like spinach and tea).
- **Family History:** A genetic predisposition to urinary stone formation.
- **Metabolic Conditions:** Disorders such as hyperparathyroidism or gout.

## Symptoms You Should Never Ignore

While small stones may pass unnoticed through the urinary tract, larger stones can block the flow of urine, causing severe complications. Seek immediate medical evaluation if you experience:

1. **Severe, sharp pain** in the lower back, side, or below the ribs that radiates to the lower abdomen and groin.
2. **Hematuria:** Pink, red, or brownish blood in your urine.
3. **Pain or a burning sensation** during urination (*Pesab polne*).
4. **Nausea and vomiting** accompanying abdominal pain.
5. **Fever and chills**, which indicate a potentially dangerous urological infection.

## Advanced Laser Surgery: RIRS and PCNL

Gone are the days when removing a kidney stone required a large surgical cut and weeks of painful hospital recovery. At National Urology Center, Dr. Arun Shah performs cutting-edge, minimally invasive laser procedures:

### Retrograde Intrarenal Surgery (RIRS)
RIRS is a revolutionary endourological procedure where a flexible ureteroscope is passed through the natural urinary opening up to the kidney—**with zero skin incisions**. A high-power Holmium laser is then used to pulverize the stone into fine dust, which is flushed out naturally.
- **Benefits:** No scars, minimal pain, same-day or next-day discharge, and rapid return to normal work.

### Percutaneous Nephrolithotomy (PCNL / Mini-PCNL)
For large or complex "staghorn" stones that cannot be cleared with RIRS alone, PCNL is the gold standard. A tiny keyhole puncture (about 1 cm) is made in the back directly into the kidney, allowing scopes and ultrasound or laser lithotripters to fragment and clear the stones completely.

## 5 Daily Habits to Prevent Kidney Stones

Once you have had a kidney stone, your risk of developing another increases significantly. Prevent future stones with these simple lifestyle modifications:

- **Drink 3 to 4 Liters of Water Daily:** Your urine should be pale and clear, not dark yellow.
- **Limit Salt Intake:** Excessive sodium triggers your kidneys to excrete more calcium into your urine.
- **Maintain Moderate Calcium Intake:** Do not completely avoid dietary calcium (like milk and curd); instead, consume it with meals to bind dietary oxalate in the intestines.
- **Reduce Animal Proteins:** Excess red meat and poultry increase uric acid production.
- **Consume Citrus Fruits:** Lemons and oranges contain dietary citrate, a natural inhibitor of crystal formation.
