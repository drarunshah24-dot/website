## What is the Prostate and Why Does it Enlarge?

The prostate is a small, walnut-sized gland located just below the urinary bladder in men, surrounding the urethra (the tube through which urine flows). Its primary function is to produce seminal fluid that nourishes and transports sperm.

With advancing age and hormonal changes, the gland gradually multiplies and grows in size. As it expands inward, it squeezes the urethra like a clamp on a garden hose, obstructing the normal flow of urine from the bladder.

## Recognizing the Early Warning Signs of BPH

If you or a loved one experience any of the following symptoms, an evaluation by an M.Ch Urologist is strongly recommended:

- **Frequent Urination:** Needing to urinate more than 8 times during the day.
- **Nocturia:** Waking up two or more times during the night to pass urine.
- **Urgent Need to Urinate:** An uncontrollable urge that is difficult to postpone.
- **Weak or Interrupted Stream:** A urine stream that starts and stops, or lacks force.
- **Difficulty Starting:** Hesitancy or straining before urine begins to flow.
- **Incomplete Emptying:** A persistent feeling that the bladder is never fully empty even after urination.
- **Post-Urination Dribbling:** Leakage of urine drops after completing urination.

## BPH vs. Prostate Cancer: Knowing the Difference

A common fear among patients is that an enlarged prostate means prostate cancer. It is crucial to understand that **BPH is benign (non-cancerous)** and does not turn into cancer. However, both conditions can exist simultaneously and share identical symptoms in their early stages.

To accurately diagnose your condition, Dr. Arun Shah conducts a thorough assessment, which includes:
1. **Digital Rectal Examination (DRE):** A clinical physical evaluation of the gland's size and texture.
2. **PSA (Prostate-Specific Antigen) Blood Test:** To screen for potential malignant changes or inflammation.
3. **Uroflowmetry:** A non-invasive test that measures the speed and volume of your urine stream.
4. **Ultrasound (USG KUB with PVR):** To check residual urine volume after urination.

## Advanced Minimally Invasive Laser Treatments

In mild cases, BPH can be effectively managed with oral medications (alpha-blockers and 5-alpha reductase inhibitors) that relax the prostate muscles and shrink the gland over time. When medications are insufficient or cause unwanted side effects, modern surgical intervention provides permanent relief.

### HoLEP (Holmium Laser Enucleation of the Prostate)
HoLEP is an advanced, endoscopic laser procedure that removes the entire obstructive tissue of an enlarged prostate without any external skin incisions.
- **Why it’s superior:** Unlike traditional open surgery or standard TURP, HoLEP can treat prostates of any size with minimal bleeding, making it safe even for elderly patients on blood thinners. Patients typically return home within 24 to 48 hours with rapid symptom relief.

### TURP (Transurethral Resection of the Prostate)
A well-established minimally invasive procedure where specialized endoscopic loops remove excess prostate tissue obstructing the urethra.
